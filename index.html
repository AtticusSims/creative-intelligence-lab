About the Lab

The Creative Intelligence Lab (CIL) is a research lab in the Department of Arts and Design, Faculty of Arts and Humanities at the University of Macau. We serve as a hub for investigating the creative potential of artificial intelligence—bridging traditional artistic practices with emerging technologies to discover new forms of expression.

The lab pursues two interconnected goals: to develop practical principles and methodologies for incorporating generative AI into art and design practice and education, and to provide a collaborative space where students and researchers can experiment with cutting-edge AI tools in the pursuit of meaningful creative work.

Through practice-based research, international collaboration, and publicly engaged creative output, the lab aims to position the University of Macau as a leader in AI-driven art and design research in the Greater Bay Area and beyond.

Vision

AI as a tool for augmenting human creativity—enhancing skills and intuition rather than replacing established artistic practices.

Beyond the Hype

Our approach to AI integration is rooted in sustainability—moving past short-lived trends to focus on the long-term impact and practical application of these technologies in creative practice.

Bridging Tradition & Innovation

We explore how AI fundamentally reshapes creative production while maintaining meaningful connections to traditional art forms, cultural heritage, and established artistic values.

Open & Adaptable

By emphasizing open-source technologies and adaptable frameworks, our research produces tools and methods that can be customized for diverse institutional, cultural, and creative contexts.

Tangible Outcomes

We move beyond theoretical research to create publicly engaged creative outputs—exhibitions, design products, publications, and public installations that directly engage with the contemporary world.

Focus Areas

01

Research & Development

Investigating new applications for AI in art and design through practice-based research. We explore questions of human-AI collaboration, develop next-generation interfaces for creative tools, and examine how AI reshapes standards of creative production—from custom-trained models to generative frameworks for artistic output.

02

Creative Production

The lab provides the resources for high-quality creative work at the intersection of AI and art: high-performance computing, open-source AI tools, digital fabrication, and professional output formats. Our work spans generative imagery, AI-driven live performance, immersive media, and large-scale installation.

03

Knowledge Exchange

We foster collaboration across disciplines and institutions through international partnerships, workshops, exhibitions, and scholarly publications. The lab actively contributes to the global discourse on AI in creative contexts through conferences, symposia, and cross-cultural projects.

AI · VR · Planetary Science · Chinese Ink Painting

Celestial Impression

Celestial Impression is a collaboration between the Creative Intelligence Lab and esteemed Hong Kong ink artist Hung Hoi. Building on the earlier Mars Impression project—in which Hung Hoi created large-scale ink paintings of the Martian surface after experiencing NASA panoramas in VR—the Celestial Impression project extends the same art-science methodology to the Moon.

The technical team develops two innovative pipelines: multi-image stitching of archival NASA photography combined with AI inpainting to produce seamless 360-degree panoramas, and AI-enhanced generation from low-resolution LROC (Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera) data using custom ComfyUI workflows to create high-fidelity lunar surface environments. These VR-ready panoramas allow Hung Hoi to immerse himself in landscapes no human has visited, translating the experience into large-scale ink paintings that bridge millennia of artistic tradition with cutting-edge technology.

People

Director

Atticus Sims

Associate Professor of Interdisciplinary Research in AI and Design

Atticus Sims is an Associate Professor of Interdisciplinary Research in Artificial Intelligence and Design at the University of Macau, within the Faculty of Arts and Humanities, Department of Arts and Design. He is an interdisciplinary researcher and educator focused on integrating AI technologies into creative practices and art and design education.

Previously a specially appointed associate professor at Kyoto Seika University’s Faculty of Media Creation, he led a president-funded investigation into pedagogical frameworks for emerging AI tools and their impact on creative practices and educational methodologies. His research examines the systematic implementation of diffusion models and generative frameworks in art and design production, with a focus on developing curricula and methodologies for AI-driven visual media creation.

This academic work is complemented by prior industry experience as the founder of KYOTO VR, an immersive media production company that created XR experiences for heritage sites and cultural institutions in Kyoto, Japan. Current projects include international collaborations exploring AI’s creative applications—such as The Metamorphosis of Plants exhibition, which investigates evolutionary principles through machine learning—and a permanent installation at Hong Kong Baptist University that visualizes knowledge structures through algorithmic composition.

He is a co-founder of Studio Pollen, an art, science and technology collective with members based across Kyoto, Macau and Hong Kong, and actively contributes to international discourse through workshops and symposia on the implementation of AI in creative contexts.
